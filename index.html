We focus on doing what we love

and being the best at what we do.

We are in the business of bringing in bliss to the lives of people through art. We want everyone to experience how art can be liberating for them by driving their passion and fostering mutual respect and compassion

Purpose

We strive and aspire to bring easy to reach, affordable learning for various folk and traditional art forms and use it as a medium to rekindle people's interest through a learning atmosphere that transcends classes.

Vision

We envision a borderless world where the art brings in positive change and becomes a greater part of people's lives.There will be lesser class difference and more people with lesser means will have the opportunity to pursue their art dreams.

Values

Passion, trust and empathy drive us and we demonstrate our beliefs to the world through our actions, content, and execution. We believe in connecting with our traditions and roots while embracing the human spirit through camaraderie and friendship.

Kamsale

Named after the bronze cymbal instrument used by the dancers, this unique folk art combines the melody of the music and narration performed by the artists in rhythm with their acrobatic dance choreography
close

Yakshagana

Literally meaning 'music of the demi-gods' and having its origins in Karnataka, it harmoniously incorporates the elements of music, dance, costume and dialogue into a total theater performance and a visual feast for the audience
close

Togalu Gombeyaata

Traditional shadow puppetry with a rich history dating back thousands of years that uses the light to cast beautiful, colorful figures onto a screen to tell a story in the form of songs to the accompaniment of music
close

Veeragase

A highly intense folk dance form native to Karnataka traditionally performed to worship Lord Shiva and Veerabhadra
close

Dollu Kunitha

One of the famous folk dances in Karnataka performed traditionally to worship the deity Beereshwara, it fuses the patterned movement of the dancers with the beats of the drum or 'Dollu'
close

Ganjifa

This ancient Indian painting style blends the intricate and dexterous artwork of hand-painted traditional playing cards with the subjects and theme reflecting the socio-religious trends
